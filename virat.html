KING KOHLI





**Virat Kohli** is an Indian cricketer widely regarded as one of the best batsmen in modern cricket. Born on **November 5, 1988**, in Delhi, India, Kohli is known for his aggressive batting style, consistency, and leadership qualities. He has achieved numerous records across all formats of the game—Test, One Day International (ODI), and T20I.

Early Life and Career:

Kohli showed an interest in cricket at a young age and quickly rose through the ranks of Delhi's age-group teams. He made his international debut for India in **August 2008** in an ODI against Sri Lanka. His early career was marked by his fiery temperament and determination to succeed, which helped him become a key player in the Indian team.

Key Achievements:

ODI Centuries

Kohli has more than **40 centuries** in ODIs, making him one of the top century-makers in the history of the format. He broke several records, including the fastest century in ODIs for India.

T20I Success

He has been consistent in T20 internationals as well, being one of the highest run-scorers in T20I cricket, with numerous match-winning performances.

Captaincy

Kohli became the **captain of the Indian cricket team** in all formats (ODI, Test, and T20I) after MS Dhoni's retirement in limited-overs cricket. Under his leadership, India achieved significant success, including a historic series win against Australia in Australia in 2018–19.

Records

Kohli holds numerous cricketing records, including being the fastest player to score **8,000**, **9,000**, **10,000**, and **11,000 runs** in ODIs.

Notable Performances:

2016 ICC T20 World Cup

Kohli's performance in the 2016 T20 World Cup was exceptional, especially his knock of **82 not out** against Australia in the semi-final.

2018–19 Border-Gavaskar Trophy

Kohli led India to a historic series win in Australia, becoming the first Indian team to win a Test series on Australian soil.

2019 Cricket World Cup

Kohli was a pivotal part of India's run to the semi-finals, although India was eliminated by New Zealand.

Batting Style:

Kohli is known for his excellent technique, exceptional wristwork, and ability to chase down tough targets under pressure. He has a reputation for **finishing games** and is often seen playing aggressive yet composed innings. His fitness and work ethic have also become legendary, with Kohli emphasizing the importance of maintaining peak physical condition for professional athletes.

Personal Life:

**Marriage**: Kohli married Bollywood actress **Anushka Sharma** in December 2017, and they have a daughter named **Vamika** (born in January 2021).**Brand Endorsements**: Kohli is a global brand ambassador for several major companies and has his own fashion brand, **Wrogn**.

Awards and Recognition:

ICC Cricketer of the Year** (multiple times).

**Padma Shri** (2017), India's fourth-highest civilian award.

**Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna** (2018), India's highest sporting honor.

**Arjuna Award** (2013) for excellence in sports.

Legacy:

Kohli's impact on Indian and world cricket is profound. Known for his passion for the game, mental toughness, and his aggressive yet humble approach, he is often compared with cricket legends like Sachin Tendulkar, Ricky Ponting, and Brian Lara. As one of the most successful and marketable cricketers of his generation, Kohli's legacy continues to inspire future generations.
